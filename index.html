Meeting #1
Nov 12, 2018
12:15 PM - 12:40 PM
Chatime Langara (6361 Cambie St)

Activities done:
During this meeting, I asked Sarah about her job. We discussed the pros and cons of working from home, as well as what her role is at Shopify and her day to day schedule. After, we went over the ENEX documents and she asked about my interests, experiences, before we began to brainstorm possible project ideas.

Approximate time spent on each activity:
Talking about her job: 15 mins
Signing Documents & Discussing ENEX: 10 mins

What I learned about my passion:
In this meeting, I learned some more specifics about what working in web development might cover, and I learned that a lot of it includes stuff I might've learned about data analytics last year for my ENEX 9. For example, Sarah brought up user data, and machine learning, and how she might see a bit of that doing what she does. This intrigued me as I hadn't figured that the two topics would relate so closely.

What I learned about myself:
In this meeting, I realized that there is a lot within web development, data science, machine learning and such that I'd like to learn about, and that I have to learn to prioritize which things would be the most practical, and important to learn. Furthermore, I have a limited amount of time for ENEX, thus I will need to choose a few small topics to learn more about and settle with that. This made me realize that I am ambitious and very eager to learn more, however lack the time to do so.

What I learned about the larger world:
As I talked to Sarah about how she works from home for a business based across the country, I learned about how, with the way that technology has developed, working from home can be just about the same as working in an office with others. However, some differences include it being a bit harder to interact with others if there are questions and concerns, since you would have to schedule a call and such. Anyhow, it is much more convenient and simple to work from home, and could be a better working environment depending on your individual ideal working conditions. Having gone over the pros and cons with Sarah, I had the opportunity to reflect on which way I would work better with and if that might be a type of job that I could potentially want to acquire in the future.

7:15 PM - 8:00 PM
2175 Pandora St

Activities done:
In this meeting, Sarah and I decided that we would start working on the framework of the site in HTML. We worked collaboratively at her house on her computer using Cloud 9, whilst having my Photoshop mockup pulled up on my own laptop. Sarah guided me and taught me how to properly format the HTML sheet, as well as which tags to use and why.

Approximate time spent on each activity:
Discussing Progress Since Last Meeting: 5 mins
Creating HTML Page: 40 mins

What I learned about my passion:
I learned that there are several stages to this process, including lots of design stages. Having completed a sketch, then a Photoshop mockup, and now an HTML frame with absolutely no content, it almost felt as if we were taking steps backwards. What I learned about myself:
I learned that I tend to learn better when seeing things done and trying them out. Having completed several HTML tutorials on freecodecamp.org, I did not completely comprehend the HTML formatting until Sarah showed me how to use which tags and when.

What I learned about the larger world:
Throughout the project, I found that I was always a bit scared to bother Sarah and ask for help, since most of it seemed to be simple concepts, yet I could not completely grasp the concepts of the use of the different languages, the different tags in HTML, and what the point of each one is. This made me realize that in the real world, it is much harder to ask for help compared to what we currently experience in school. As you get older and are expected to know more, often it can be intimidating to admit that you don't understand and need help or guidance, but putting pride and fear aside to ask questions can often be what you need to succeed.

Meeting #7

April 29, 2019
5:30 PM - 7 PM
2175 Pandora St

Activities done:
Now that we had a basic setup of most features, we started to make these features resemble my original site a bit more & and debug. Our main goals from here on were to make the site function smoothly, to incorporate all the functions I wanted it to, and debug.

Approximate time spent on each activity:
Editing the site and debugging: 1.5 Hours

What I learned about my passion:
I learned from this session that debugging can be very tedious and time-consuming. within this session, most of our time was spent trying to figure out why some functions would not work as expected, or would not run at all, and figure out where it went wrong. This could be anything from missing a bracket, or the C9 site just glitching. This turned out to be very difficult for us to work with, and made it harder for me to work on the site in my own time, since I don't have as much experience to be able to find the source of the bugs as easily.

What I learned about myself:
I felt that in this session, I was quite impatient. It felt like we weren't making much progress, since we had to spend so much time playing around with different parts to find the bugs, and make our site work. In addition, we weren't able to get through the whole site in this session and had to schedule another session to continue debugging and filling in the holes in my site. This felt frustrating and made me a little upset with the fact that things weren't going as planned, and had to be delayed so much.

What I learned about the larger world:
Struggling to fix these issues taught me that it takes time to achieve greatness, and that perfection isn't possible. Things won't always go as planned, and to be happy or have success, it is important to be adaptable and be able to change your goals. My expectations of my final project throughout this process had definitely changed and been adapted several times, and continues to change. I don't believe that this project is as perfect as it could be, and I don't think it's what I expected it to be, but considering how much time and effort that Sarah and I put in to it, I'd say I'm very happy to see where it is now, and that the way that I can say that is because I was able to be reasonable with my expectations for myself.

This was quite hard for me to figure out and adapt to, since it seemed like I had so many issues that I wanted to deal with all at once.
